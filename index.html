Description List

Red-tailed tropicbird:
The red-tailed tropicbird is a seabird that nests across the Indian and Pacific Oceans. It nests in colonies on oceanic islands. The species is also known by its Maori name, amokura.
Black-necked stork:
The black-necked stork is a tall long-necked wading bird in the stork family. It is a resident species across the Indian Subcontinent and Southeast Asia with a disjunct population in Australia.It is one of the few storks that is strongly territorial when feeding.
Eurasian spoonbill:
The Eurasian spoonbill or common spoonbill is a wading bird of the ibis and spoonbill family Threskiornithidae.
Little bittern:
The little bittern or common little bittern is a wading bird in the heron family, Ardeidae. This bittern is native to the Old World, breeding in Africa, central and southern Europe, western and southern Asia, and Madagascar.
Eastern great egret:
The eastern great egret , a white heron in the genus Ardea, is usually considered a subspecies of the great egret . It was first described by British ornithologist John Edward Gray in 1831.
